Hi there, I'm Cem!

I'm a PhD candidate at Tinbergen Institute and Erasmus School of Economics, Erasmus University Rotterdam.

My research proposes Bayesian mechanisms to elicit and aggregate subjective judgments effectively.

The mechanisms are applicable in forecasting, belief elicitation, utilizing expertise, harnessing the wisdom of crowds etc.

I use insights and tools from Decision Theory, Experimental Economics and Behavioral Economics.

My Work

Extracting the Wisdom of Crowds in probabilistic judgments (job market paper)

Incentives for self-extremized expert judgments to alleviate the shared-information problem (under review at Decision)

Teaching Experience



My teaching interests:

  • Microeconomics
  • Behavioral Economics, Biases in Judgment and Decision Making
  • Experimental Economics
  • Quantitative Methods / Statistics (in particular using R and Python)

At Erasmus School of Economics, I teach Applied Behavioral Economics in our masters' programme. I supervise experimental projects that apply behavioral insights in practical problems.

I also regularly supervise bachelors' and masters' theses in Behavioral Economics.


In the past, I have been a TA in various courses in Tinbergen Institute and Bogazici University, Turkey.
